How Airplanes Fly

Introduction

Airplanes fly using the principles of aerodynamics. They must overcome four forces: lift, weight, thrust, and drag.

Forces Acting on an Airplane

Force Description Image
Lift Lift is created by the airplane's wings. It works against the weight to keep the airplane in the sky.
Weight Weight is the force of gravity pulling the airplane down.
Thrust Thrust is the force that moves the airplane forward. It's usually provided by the engines.
Drag Drag is the resistance an airplane faces while moving through the air.
